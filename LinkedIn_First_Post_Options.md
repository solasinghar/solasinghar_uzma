🔬 My research on AI safety in deployed medical devices is now published!

After months of experimental validation, I'm excited to share:

"Monitoring Population Drift in Deployed AI Medical Devices: An AI-Centred Diagnostic Accuracy Study"

📊 **The Core Problem:**
When you train an AI model on one patient population, then deploy it to a different hospital or region, 
something unexpected happens: the model's accuracy drops. The data has drifted. The patients are different. 
The model doesn't know how to handle it.

**The Research Question:**
Can we detect when this is happening? And what's the best way to catch both sudden AND gradual drift?

🔍 **Key Findings:**
• Distance-based anomaly detection (One-Class SVM) is 3.18× better at catching sudden changes
• Partitioning-based methods (Isolation Forest) are 4.40× better at detecting gradual degradation
• **No algorithm wins both ways** — this is a critical trade-off

💡 **Why It Matters:**
In healthcare, silent failures are worse than no AI at all. If your deployed medical AI is drifting and 
you don't know, you've created a patient safety risk. This research provides a monitoring framework to 
detect when drift happens.
